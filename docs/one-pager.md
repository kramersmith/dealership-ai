# Dealership AI — One Pager

## The Problem

Car buying is broken for everyone. Buyers get overcharged an average of $2,000 per transaction through unauthorized add-ons, inflated interest rates, and confusing deal structures. Meanwhile, 75% of salespeople only know one close, and dealerships lose deals they should win because reps can't handle objections or capture customer intelligence.

No tool exists for the in-person showroom. Everything on the market today — Gong, Balto, Cresta, Dealertrack — is built for VoIP call centers or post-deal compliance. Nobody is helping either side in the moment that matters most: when the buyer and salesperson are face to face.

## The Solution

Two separate AI-powered smartphone apps — one for buyers, one for dealerships.

**The buyer app** is a real-time negotiation assistant. Before the visit, it analyzes vehicle history, builds a game plan, and sets target prices. At the dealership, it decodes deal sheets from photos, validates APR against the buyer's credit profile, coaches negotiation in real time, and tells the buyer when to walk away. A persistent dashboard keeps target prices, checklists, and deal status visible at all times — so the buyer stays rational under pressure.

**The dealer app** starts with AI training simulations where salespeople practice against realistic customer scenarios. Over time, it expands into real-time objection coaching, automated CRM capture, manager tools, and F&I compliance monitoring.

Both apps get smarter with every deal. Anonymized outcome data — what tactics worked, which dealers negotiate, callback rates after walk-aways — builds a data moat that no competitor can replicate from scratch.

## Why Now

- The FTC warned 97 auto dealer groups about deceptive pricing in March 2026. Regulatory pressure is intensifying.
- 68-80% of customers are charged for products they never consented to (per recent FTC settlements).
- 76% of buyers don't trust dealerships. Demand for transparency tools is at an all-time high.
- Multimodal AI (vision + language) is now good enough to analyze deal sheets, interpret vehicle histories, and coach negotiations — all from a phone.

## What Makes This Different

- **In-person, real-time** — not post-deal, not VoIP. This is for the showroom floor.
- **Both sides of the table** — two separate products with no data sharing between them. Each side gets a tool that makes them better.
- **Smartphone-first** — no hardware installation, no setup friction. Download and go.
- **Data network effect** — every deal makes the AI smarter for the next buyer and the next salesperson.

## Business Model

- **Buyer app:** Consumer subscription (pricing TBD). Potential for freemium entry via Deal Decoder.
- **Dealer app:** Per-seat or per-store subscription. Training simulations as the initial hook.
- **Future revenue:** Lender partnerships, data licensing, mechanic/inspection network, aftermarket warranty marketplace.

## Traction

- Founder validated the concept during a real truck purchase (March 2026), using ChatGPT as a live buying assistant over 5+ hours. The AI caught a 9.99% interest rate markup on an 800 credit score — saving ~$2,000-$3,000 on a single deal.
- Pitched the idea to a salesman during that purchase. Immediate interest.
- First-to-market in the in-person automotive AI space.

## MVP

Chat + voice + photo upload for the buyer app. AI training simulations for the dealer app. No audio recording, no hardware, no integrations needed. Works everywhere from day one.
